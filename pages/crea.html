Editorial Design

CREA, paper and digital magazine

Explore the project on Behance

Project

Project II/UOC

Date

2022

Challenge

We are developing an editorial project to launch CREA, a magazine dedicated to exploring creativity from a gender perspective. The initial plan includes a premium monthly print edition of approximately 80 pages with national distribution, complemented by a digital version.

Role

As part of this fictional editorial project, developed within a graphic design training exercise, I took on the role of art director and editorial designer. My responsibilities included defining the magazine’s visual identity, establishing the layout and typographic style, selecting and editing images, and ensuring consistency between content and design. I also participated in conceptualizing the magazine’s theme and structure, translating the editorial vision into a visually engaging and professional publication, both in print and digital formats.

Solution

CREA is a premium monthly cultural magazine with national reach that explores creativity from a gender perspective. With approximately 80 pages in its print edition and an enriched digital version, the publication combines high-quality journalism, meticulous editorial design, and a striking visual proposal. Its goal is to give voice to artists, thinkers, and cultural agents who challenge established narratives, showcasing projects and ideas that broaden our understanding of innovation and creative expression in fields such as art, design, literature, science, and technology. With an inclusive, elegant, and thoughtful tone, CREA is aimed at a diverse community of readers — from professionals and students in the creative industries to culture enthusiasts interested in diversity and equity. Through interviews, essays, visual portfolios, and multimedia content, the magazine seeks to become a cultural reference point that connects local talent with a global dialogue, positioning creativity as a tool for social change.
