Project

Prototype Training Project

Date

2025

Overview

This project was developed as part of a Figma prototyping course, with the goal of designing and validating a digital application from its initial concept to an interactive version ready for user testing. The work included defining the app’s structure, specifying key functionalities, and developing a user experience that is coherent and centered on the needs of the target audience. A low-fidelity prototype was created to explore the information architecture and interaction flows, which was then tested with real users. Based on the feedback received, strategic adjustments were made to optimize both usability and visual design. In addition, a UI kit was produced, featuring a predefined set of visual elements and reusable interface components designed to be adapted for building web and mobile applications. This visual system ensures design consistency and streamlines future development processes.

Role

Responsible for the end-to-end prototyping process, including defining the app’s structure and functionalities, designing the user experience, creating the visual interface, and developing a reusable UI kit. Conducted user testing on the low-fidelity prototype and iterated the design based on feedback to enhance usability and visual appeal.

Challenge

Today, many people live under high levels of stress due to work demands, hyperconnectivity, and the fast pace of everyday life. Students, professionals, and adults in general, aged 20 to 6, are constantly exposed to stimuli and demands that make it difficult to find moments of calm. Although they are aware of the importance of their mental health, they often lack accessible tools to help them disconnect and reduce stress.

Solution

Develop a mobile application focused on providing moments of disconnection through breathing exercises, meditation practices, and resources that promote calm. The app offers guided sessions, reminders, and visual and auditory tools to help users take mindful breaks throughout the day, improving mental well-being and reducing the overload caused by hyperconnectivity.
