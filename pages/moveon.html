Project

UX/UI Design Training Project

Date

2025

Overview

This project was developed as part of a UX/UI Design training program, applying User-Centered Design and Design Thinking methodologies to create an app that promotes sustainable urban mobility. Through user research, we identified the needs of adults living in cities—students, professionals, and older adults—who seek eco-friendly transportation options, wish to reduce their carbon footprint, and stay physically active without focusing on high-performance goals. The result is a solution that enables personal tracking, goal setting, and friendly competition.

Role

I was responsible for leading the end-to-end UX/UI design process, from user research and needs analysis to ideation, prototyping, and usability testing. I applied User-Centered Design and Design Thinking methodologies to ensure the final solution addressed real user needs. This included creating user personas, user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes, as well as iterating based on feedback.

Challenge

Urban residents often face limited sustainable mobility options that balance convenience, environmental impact, and physical activity. Many eco-conscious individuals want to reduce their carbon footprint and stay active, but lack engaging, user-friendly tools to track their progress, set achievable goals, and connect with like-minded peers. The challenge was to design an intuitive, motivating solution that addresses these needs while appealing to a broad demographic—from students and professionals to older adults.

Solution

We designed a mobile app to deliver an engaging and intuitive experience for eco-conscious urban residents. The app enables users to track their mobility activities, set personal goals, monitor their environmental impact, and engage in friendly challenges with peers. Through a clean, accessible interface and a focus on motivating interactions, the solution encourages sustainable habits while promoting physical activity without a high-performance focus.
